# Business Process: Educational Platform for Minors with Behavioral Analytics

## Purpose
This process operates an online educational platform for children aged 5-17, collecting learning data, behavioral patterns, and personal information to personalize educational content, track academic progress, and share insights with parents, schools, and educational content partners. The platform also monetizes anonymized learning data by selling it to educational research firms and EdTech companies.

## Processing Activities

### Data Collection
Primary systems:
- **Student Learning Platform**: Collects StudentID, FullName, DateOfBirth, Grade Level, and ParentGuardianContactInfo
- **Learning Analytics Engine**: Tracks LearningProgress, QuizScores, TimeSpentPerSubject, StruggleAreas, and SkillMastery
- **Behavioral Monitoring System**: Captures ClickPatterns, AttentionSpan, FrustrationIndicators, and EngagementMetrics
- **Content Interaction Tracker**: Records VideosWatched, GamesPlayed, BooksRead, and PreferredLearningStyles
- **Device and Usage Logs**: Collects DeviceID, IPAddress, LocationData, and SessionDuration

### Parental Consent Management
The **Consent Management Platform** stores:
- Parent/Guardian Full Name
- Parent Email Address
- Parent Phone Number
- Consent Records (for data collection, third-party sharing, marketing)
- Verification of parental authority

### Personalization and Profiling
The **Student Profiling Engine** creates detailed profiles including:
- Academic strengths and weaknesses
- Learning pace and style preferences
- Behavioral patterns and attention metrics
- Predicted future performance
- Recommended learning paths

### Cross-Border Data Transfers
Student data is transferred internationally:
- **US to India**: Customer support and content moderation services (receives StudentID, LearningProgress, and ParentContactInfo)
- **EU to US**: Centralized data warehouse for analytics (requires parental consent under GDPR Article 8)
- **Global Content Delivery**: Student interaction data synced across regional servers for performance

### Third-Party Data Sharing
Student data shared with:
- **EducationalResearch Institute** (US-based): Receives anonymized learning data including QuizScores, TimeSpentPerSubject, and DemographicInfo (Age, Grade, Location) for educational research
- **ContentCreators Network** (Multi-national): Receives aggregated engagement metrics to optimize educational content
- **SchoolDistrict Partners**: Receives individual student progress reports including LearningProgress, SkillMastery, and BehavioralMetrics for students enrolled through school programs
- **AdTech for Kids Inc**: Receives anonymized behavioral segments for age-appropriate advertising (requires parental consent)
- **AI Tutoring Solutions** (Canada-based): Receives student learning data to train adaptive learning algorithms

### Automated Decision Making
The platform makes automated decisions on:
- Content difficulty adjustments
- Learning path recommendations
- Intervention triggers for struggling students
- Reward and gamification elements
- Content filtering based on age appropriateness

### Data Subject Types

- Parents/Guardians
- Teachers (for school-integrated programs)

## Legal Basis
- Parental Consent (primary basis for processing children's data under COPPA and GDPR Article 8)
- Contractual Necessity (for service delivery)
- Legitimate Interest (for platform security and fraud prevention)

## Special Protections for Children
- Age verification mechanisms
- Parental consent required for users under 13 (COPPA) / 16 (GDPR)
- No behavioral advertising without explicit parental consent
- Simplified privacy notices for children
- Enhanced security measures for children's data
- Prohibition on selling children's data without consent
- Regular privacy audits focused on child safety

## Cross-Border Transfer Mechanisms
- Standard Contractual Clauses with additional child data safeguards
- Parental consent for international transfers
- Privacy Shield certification (where applicable)
- Binding Corporate Rules with child-specific provisions

## Data Subject Rights
**For Parents/Guardians:**
- Right to access all data collected about their child
- Right to delete child's account and all associated data
- Right to withdraw consent at any time
- Right to object to automated decision-making
- Right to restrict data sharing with third parties
- Right to data portability

**For Children:**
- Age-appropriate privacy controls
- Ability to request parental review of data
- Right to be forgotten upon reaching age of majority

## Retention Period
- Active student accounts: Duration of subscription + 1 year
- Learning progress data: 3 years from last activity (for longitudinal studies)
- Parental consent records: Duration of child's use + 3 years
- Anonymized research data: Indefinite
- Deleted account data: Purged within 30 days (except legal holds)

## Data Protection Measures
- Encryption of all children's data (AES-256)
- Separate database for children under 13
- Enhanced access controls (need-to-know basis)
- Regular security audits and penetration testing
- Data minimization (collect only necessary data)
- Privacy by design and default
- Incident response plan specific to child data breaches

## Compliance Requirements
- **COPPA (US)**: Verifiable parental consent, privacy policy, data deletion rights
- **GDPR Article 8 (EU)**: Parental consent for children under 16
- **CCPA/CPRA (California)**: Enhanced protections for minors under 16
- **UK Age Appropriate Design Code**: 15 standards for online services for children
- **FERPA (US)**: For school-integrated programs handling educational records

## Transparency and Communication
- Child-friendly privacy policy (simple language, visual aids)
- Parent dashboard showing all collected data
- Regular privacy updates to parents
- Clear opt-out mechanisms for data sharing
- Annual privacy review with parents
- Notification of any material changes to data practices

## Ethical Considerations
- Minimize data collection from children
- Avoid manipulative design patterns
- Protect children from targeted advertising
- Ensure age-appropriate content filtering
- Balance personalization with privacy
- Consider developmental impact of data collection
- Engage with child advocacy groups
- Regular ethical reviews of AI/ML models used on children's data
