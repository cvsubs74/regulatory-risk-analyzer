BUSINESS PROCESS: Customer Onboarding and KYC Verification
===========================================================

Process Owner: Customer Operations Team
Last Updated: November 2025
Regulatory Framework: GDPR, CCPA, KYC/AML Requirements

PROCESS OVERVIEW
----------------
This process describes how we onboard new customers for our financial services platform, 
including identity verification, risk assessment, and account setup.

DATA ASSETS USED
----------------

1. CUSTOMER REGISTRATION DATABASE (CRM-DB-001)
   Location: AWS RDS PostgreSQL (us-east-1)
   Purpose: Store customer profile and registration data
   Personal Data Collected:
   - Full Name (required for identity verification)
   - Email Address (primary communication channel)
   - Phone Number (two-factor authentication)
   - Date of Birth (age verification, regulatory requirement)
   - Residential Address (jurisdiction determination, compliance)
   - Nationality (sanctions screening, regulatory compliance)
   - Government ID Number (KYC verification)
   - Tax Identification Number (tax reporting obligations)
   
   Legal Basis: Contract performance, Legal obligation (KYC/AML)
   Retention Period: 7 years after account closure (regulatory requirement)

2. IDENTITY VERIFICATION SERVICE (ID-VERIFY-SVC)
   Provider: Jumio Identity Verification API
   Purpose: Verify customer identity documents
   Personal Data Shared:
   - Government-issued ID images (passport, driver's license)
   - Facial biometric data (selfie for liveness detection)
   - Document metadata (issue date, expiry, document number)
   
   Data Processing: Real-time verification, no long-term storage by us
   Third-Party Storage: Jumio retains data per their retention policy
   Legal Basis: Legal obligation (KYC), Legitimate interest (fraud prevention)

3. CREDIT SCORING SYSTEM (CREDIT-SCORE-API)
   Provider: Experian Credit Bureau API
   Purpose: Assess creditworthiness and fraud risk
   Personal Data Shared:
   - Full Name
   - Date of Birth
   - Social Security Number / National ID
   - Current and Previous Addresses
   - Employment Information
   
   Legal Basis: Legitimate interest (risk assessment), Consent
   Data Minimization: Only share data necessary for credit check

4. SANCTIONS SCREENING DATABASE (SANCTIONS-DB)
   Provider: Dow Jones Risk & Compliance
   Purpose: Screen against OFAC, UN, EU sanctions lists
   Personal Data Processed:
   - Full Name
   - Date of Birth
   - Nationality
   - Passport Number
   
   Legal Basis: Legal obligation (AML/CTF regulations)
   Automated Decision: Yes - account creation blocked if match found

5. CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION PLATFORM (EMAIL-SVC)
   Provider: SendGrid
   Purpose: Send onboarding emails and notifications
   Personal Data Shared:
   - Email Address
   - First Name (for personalization)
   - Account Status
   
   Legal Basis: Contract performance
   Opt-out: Marketing emails only (transactional emails required)

6. DOCUMENT STORAGE SYSTEM (DOC-VAULT)
   Location: AWS S3 with encryption (us-east-1)
   Purpose: Store copies of identity documents
   Personal Data Stored:
   - Scanned ID documents (passport, driver's license)
   - Proof of address documents (utility bills, bank statements)
   - Signed customer agreements
   
   Security: AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS in transit
   Access Control: Role-based, audit logged
   Retention: 7 years (regulatory requirement)

PROCESS FLOW
------------

Step 1: Initial Registration
- Customer submits registration form via web application
- Data collected: Name, Email, Phone, Password
- Data stored in: CRM-DB-001
- Purpose: Create user account, enable login

Step 2: Identity Verification
- Customer uploads government ID and takes selfie
- Data sent to: ID-VERIFY-SVC (Jumio)
- Biometric comparison performed
- Result stored in: CRM-DB-001 (verification status only)
- Original documents stored in: DOC-VAULT
- Purpose: Comply with KYC regulations, prevent identity fraud

Step 3: Personal Information Collection
- Customer provides detailed personal information
- Data collected: DOB, Address, Nationality, Tax ID, SSN
- Data stored in: CRM-DB-001
- Purpose: Regulatory compliance, tax reporting

Step 4: Credit and Risk Assessment
- System queries CREDIT-SCORE-API with customer data
- Credit score and risk indicators retrieved
- Data stored in: CRM-DB-001 (score and risk level only)
- Purpose: Determine account limits, pricing tier

Step 5: Sanctions Screening
- Automated check against SANCTIONS-DB
- Customer data matched against watchlists
- If match found: Account creation blocked, compliance team notified
- Result logged in: CRM-DB-001
- Purpose: Comply with AML/CTF regulations

Step 6: Account Approval
- Manual review by compliance team (if flagged)
- Automated approval (if all checks pass)
- Decision recorded in: CRM-DB-001
- Purpose: Final compliance verification

Step 7: Welcome Communication
- Welcome email sent via EMAIL-SVC
- Includes account details and next steps
- Purpose: Customer communication, contract performance

DATA SHARING AND TRANSFERS
---------------------------

International Transfers:
- Jumio (ID verification): Data processed in EU and US
- Experian: Data processed in US
- SendGrid: Data processed in US
- Safeguards: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), adequacy decisions

Third-Party Processors:
- AWS (infrastructure): Data Processing Agreement in place
- Jumio: Data Processing Agreement, certified under Privacy Shield successor
- Experian: Data Processing Agreement
- Dow Jones: Data Processing Agreement
- SendGrid: Data Processing Agreement

INDIVIDUAL RIGHTS
-----------------

Right to Access: Customers can request copy of their data via support portal
Right to Rectification: Customers can update information in account settings
Right to Erasure: Available after account closure (subject to retention requirements)
Right to Restrict Processing: Available for disputed data
Right to Data Portability: Export available in JSON format
Right to Object: Available for marketing communications
Automated Decision-Making: Credit decisions can be reviewed by human

SECURITY MEASURES
-----------------

Technical Measures:
- Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Multi-factor authentication for employee access
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Regular security audits and penetration testing
- Automated vulnerability scanning

Organizational Measures:
- Employee background checks
- Confidentiality agreements
- Security awareness training
- Incident response procedures
- Data breach notification process (72 hours)

COMPLIANCE NOTES
----------------

GDPR Compliance:
- Legal basis documented for each processing activity
- Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) completed
- Privacy notices provided at collection
- Consent obtained where required
- Data minimization principles applied

CCPA Compliance:
- Privacy policy discloses data collection and sharing
- Opt-out mechanisms for data sales (N/A - we don't sell data)
- Do Not Sell My Personal Information link provided
- Consumer rights request process established

KYC/AML Compliance:
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) performed
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers
- Ongoing monitoring for suspicious activity
- Record retention per regulatory requirements (7 years)

RISKS AND MITIGATION
--------------------

Risk: Unauthorized access to identity documents
Mitigation: Encryption, access controls, audit logging, regular security reviews

Risk: Data breach at third-party processor
Mitigation: Vendor due diligence, DPAs, contractual liability, insurance

Risk: Non-compliance with KYC regulations
Mitigation: Automated checks, compliance team review, regular audits

Risk: Excessive data retention
Mitigation: Automated deletion after retention period, regular data audits

Risk: Inadequate consent for data processing
Mitigation: Clear privacy notices, granular consent options, consent management platform
