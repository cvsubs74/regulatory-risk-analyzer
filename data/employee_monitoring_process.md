# Employee Monitoring and Surveillance Process

## Process Overview
**Process ID:** PROC-EM-002  
**Department:** Human Resources & IT Security  
**Owner:** Chief Human Resources Officer  
**Last Updated:** November 2025

## Purpose
This process describes the organization's employee monitoring and surveillance practices to ensure productivity, prevent data loss, and maintain security.

## Monitoring Technologies Deployed

### 1. Computer and Network Monitoring
- **Keystroke Logging**: All keystrokes on company devices are logged and stored
- **Screen Recording**: Random screenshots taken every 3-10 minutes
- **Application Monitoring**: All applications used and time spent tracked
- **Website Monitoring**: Complete browsing history including personal sites
- **Email Monitoring**: All emails (sent and received) are scanned and archived
- **File Access Tracking**: Every file opened, modified, or deleted is logged
- **USB Device Tracking**: All USB connections and file transfers monitored

### 2. Video Surveillance
- **Office Cameras**: 150+ cameras throughout office spaces including:
  - Open work areas and cubicles
  - Break rooms and cafeterias
  - Hallways and entrances
  - Parking lots
  - Some cameras with audio recording capability
  
- **Facial Recognition**: AI-powered facial recognition tracks:
  - Employee arrival and departure times
  - Movement patterns throughout building
  - Time spent away from desk
  - Interactions with other employees

### 3. Location Tracking
- **Badge Tracking**: RFID badges track employee location within building
- **Mobile Device GPS**: Company phones tracked 24/7 including:
  - Real-time location monitoring
  - Location history stored indefinitely
  - Geofencing alerts when employees leave designated areas
  
- **Vehicle Tracking**: GPS trackers on company vehicles and personal vehicles used for business

### 4. Biometric Monitoring
- **Fingerprint Scanners**: Required for building access and computer login
- **Facial Recognition**: Used for authentication and attendance tracking
- **Voice Recognition**: Phone calls analyzed for voice patterns and stress levels
- **Wellness Monitoring**: Fitness tracker data collected from voluntary wellness program

### 5. Communication Monitoring
- **Phone Calls**: All desk phone calls recorded and transcribed
- **Mobile Calls**: Company mobile phone calls monitored and recorded
- **Instant Messages**: All Slack, Teams, and chat messages archived and analyzed
- **Video Conferences**: Zoom and Teams meetings recorded without participant notification
- **Social Media**: Employee social media accounts monitored for company mentions

## Data Collection and Analysis

### Productivity Metrics
Automated systems track and score employees on:
- **Active Time**: Keyboard and mouse activity measured per hour
- **Application Usage**: Time spent in productive vs. non-productive applications
- **Website Categories**: Categorization of all websites visited
- **Email Response Time**: Average time to respond to emails
- **Meeting Attendance**: Punctuality and participation in meetings
- **Idle Time**: Time away from computer or inactive

### Behavioral Analytics
AI systems analyze employee behavior for:
- **Productivity Patterns**: Identification of high and low productivity periods
- **Collaboration Patterns**: Analysis of who employees interact with
- **Sentiment Analysis**: Emotional tone in emails and messages
- **Stress Indicators**: Detection of stress through typing patterns and communication
- **Flight Risk**: Prediction of employees likely to leave the company
- **Misconduct Prediction**: Identification of employees at risk of policy violations

### Performance Scoring
Automated performance scores generated based on:
- Productivity metrics (40%)
- Collaboration and communication (25%)
- Attendance and punctuality (20%)
- Compliance with policies (15%)

Scores used for:
- Performance reviews
- Promotion decisions
- Compensation adjustments
- Termination decisions

## Remote Work Monitoring

### Work-from-Home Surveillance
For remote employees:
- **Webcam Monitoring**: Periodic webcam photos to verify presence
- **Activity Monitoring**: Continuous tracking of computer activity
- **Productivity Software**: Mandatory installation of monitoring software on personal devices
- **Virtual Private Network (VPN)**: All internet traffic routed through company VPN for monitoring

### Personal Device Monitoring
Employees using personal devices for work must:
- Install Mobile Device Management (MDM) software
- Grant access to:
  - All files and documents
  - Location data
  - Contact lists
  - Photos and media
  - Browsing history
  - Personal apps and usage

## Data Retention and Storage

### Retention Periods
- **Keystroke Logs**: 3 years
- **Screen Recordings**: 2 years
- **Email Archives**: 7 years
- **Video Surveillance**: 90 days (flagged incidents: indefinite)
- **Location Data**: 5 years
- **Biometric Data**: Duration of employment + 7 years
- **Performance Scores**: Indefinite

### Data Access
Access to employee monitoring data:
- HR Department: Full access to all data
- Direct Managers: Access to their team's productivity data
- IT Security: Full access for security investigations
- Legal Department: Access upon request
- Third-Party Vendors: Analytics vendors have access to anonymized data

## Employee Rights and Transparency

### Notice to Employees
- **Onboarding**: General mention of monitoring in employee handbook
- **Consent**: Bundled with employment agreement (condition of employment)
- **Ongoing Notice**: No regular updates about monitoring practices
- **Specific Monitoring**: No notice when specific monitoring is activated

### Employee Access Rights
- **Data Access**: Employees can request their monitoring data (30-day response time)
- **Correction**: Limited ability to correct inaccurate data
- **Deletion**: No deletion rights while employed
- **Objection**: No right to object to monitoring (condition of employment)

### Opt-Out Options
- **No Opt-Out**: Monitoring is mandatory condition of employment
- **Personal Devices**: Can decline to use personal devices (but limits job functions)
- **Wellness Program**: Voluntary but incentivized with insurance discounts

## Use of Monitoring Data

### Disciplinary Actions
Monitoring data used for:
- **Performance Improvement Plans**: Based on productivity scores
- **Written Warnings**: For policy violations detected through monitoring
- **Termination**: Automated recommendations for termination based on scores
- **Legal Proceedings**: Evidence in employment disputes

### Algorithmic Management
Automated systems make decisions about:
- **Task Assignment**: AI assigns tasks based on productivity patterns
- **Schedule Optimization**: Shifts assigned based on productivity data
- **Break Reminders**: Forced breaks when productivity drops
- **Coaching Interventions**: Automatic coaching sessions triggered by low scores

### Third-Party Sharing
Employee data shared with:
- **Background Check Providers**: For ongoing monitoring
- **Insurance Companies**: Wellness data for premium calculations
- **Recruitment Agencies**: Performance data for contract workers
- **Law Enforcement**: Upon request without warrant requirement

## Special Categories of Data

### Health Information
Collected through:
- Wellness program participation
- Sick leave requests and medical documentation
- Disability accommodation requests
- Stress indicators from behavioral analytics
- Ergonomic assessment data

### Union Activity
Monitoring specifically tracks:
- Attendance at union meetings (via location data)
- Communication with union representatives
- Discussion of union topics in emails/messages
- Employee organizing activities

### Protected Characteristics
Data collection may reveal:
- Religious beliefs (prayer times, religious websites)
- Political opinions (social media, news consumption)
- Pregnancy status (medical appointments, searches)
- Disability status (accommodation requests, work patterns)

## Compliance Risks and Issues

### GDPR Violations (EU Employees)
1. **Lack of Legal Basis**: Monitoring exceeds what's necessary for employment
2. **Excessive Data Collection**: Violates data minimization principle
3. **Inadequate Consent**: Consent not freely given (condition of employment)
4. **Special Category Data**: Health and union data processed without proper safeguards
5. **Automated Decision Making**: No human oversight of algorithmic decisions
6. **Transparency**: Inadequate information about monitoring practices
7. **Data Subject Rights**: Limited access, correction, and deletion rights

### US Employment Law Issues
1. **National Labor Relations Act (NLRA)**: Monitoring may chill protected concerted activity
2. **Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)**: Health data collection may violate ADA
3. **Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA)**: Some monitoring may exceed legal limits
4. **State Wiretapping Laws**: Two-party consent states require notification
5. **Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA)**: Wellness data may include genetic info

### State-Specific Privacy Laws
1. **California**: CCPA applies to employee data
   - Must provide notice of data collection
   - Must honor deletion requests (with exceptions)
   - Cannot discriminate for exercising privacy rights

2. **New York**: Workplace monitoring laws require:
   - Prior written notice of electronic monitoring
   - Notice posted in conspicuous location

3. **Connecticut**: Electronic monitoring law requires:
   - Prior written notice to employees
   - Notice of types of monitoring

4. **Delaware**: Workplace privacy law restricts:
   - Email monitoring without notice
   - Social media monitoring

### International Compliance Issues
1. **EU Member States**: Additional national laws on workplace monitoring
2. **UK**: ICO guidance on monitoring requires proportionality
3. **Canada**: PIPEDA requires consent and limits on monitoring
4. **Australia**: Privacy Act limits on employee monitoring

## Ethical and Reputational Risks

### Employee Trust and Morale
- Pervasive monitoring creates culture of distrust
- Reduced employee autonomy and job satisfaction
- Increased stress and anxiety
- Potential for increased turnover

### Discrimination Risks
- Algorithmic bias in performance scoring
- Disparate impact on protected classes
- Disability discrimination through productivity metrics
- Pregnancy discrimination through pattern detection

### Data Security Risks
- Large volume of sensitive employee data creates attractive target
- Insider threat from employees with access to monitoring data
- Third-party vendor access increases breach risk
- Biometric data breach could enable identity theft

## Recommended Remediation Actions

### Immediate Actions
1. **Conduct Privacy Impact Assessment**: Evaluate necessity and proportionality of all monitoring
2. **Review Legal Basis**: Ensure legitimate basis for each type of monitoring
3. **Enhance Transparency**: Provide clear, detailed notice of all monitoring practices
4. **Implement Safeguards**: Add human oversight to automated decisions
5. **Limit Special Category Data**: Stop collecting health and union-related data
6. **Update Policies**: Revise employee handbook and privacy notices
7. **Train Managers**: Educate on proper use of monitoring data
8. **Implement Access Controls**: Limit who can access monitoring data

### Medium-Term Actions
1. **Reduce Data Collection**: Eliminate unnecessary monitoring
2. **Implement Data Minimization**: Collect only what's needed for legitimate purposes
3. **Shorten Retention**: Reduce retention periods for monitoring data
4. **Enhance Employee Rights**: Implement robust access, correction, deletion processes
5. **Anonymous Feedback**: Create channels for employees to raise privacy concerns
6. **Third-Party Audits**: Engage external auditors to assess compliance
7. **Vendor Management**: Review and limit third-party access to employee data

### Long-Term Strategic Changes
1. **Trust-Based Culture**: Shift from surveillance to trust-based management
2. **Outcome-Based Performance**: Focus on results rather than activity monitoring
3. **Employee Participation**: Involve employees in monitoring policy decisions
4. **Privacy by Design**: Build privacy into HR systems and processes
5. **Ethical AI**: Implement fairness and bias testing for algorithmic systems
6. **Competitive Advantage**: Position privacy-respecting practices as talent attraction tool
