BUSINESS PROCESS: Patient Health Analytics and Predictive Care Management
==========================================================================

Process Owner: Clinical Analytics Team
Last Updated: November 2025
Regulatory Framework: HIPAA, HITECH Act, GDPR (for EU patients), State Privacy Laws

PROCESS OVERVIEW
----------------
This process describes how we collect, analyze, and use patient health data to 
provide predictive analytics, care recommendations, and population health management 
for our healthcare network. This includes integration of EHR data, claims data, 
wearable device data, and social determinants of health.

DATA ASSETS USED
----------------

1. ELECTRONIC HEALTH RECORD SYSTEM (EHR-MAIN)
   Provider: Epic Systems
   Location: On-premise data center (HIPAA-compliant)
   Purpose: Store comprehensive patient medical records
   Protected Health Information (PHI) Stored:
   - Patient Demographics:
     * Full Name, Date of Birth, Gender
     * Social Security Number
     * Address, Phone Number, Email
     * Emergency Contact Information
     * Race, Ethnicity (for health disparities research)
     * Preferred Language
     * Marital Status, Employment Status
   
   - Clinical Data:
     * Medical History (diagnoses, conditions, allergies)
     * Medication Lists (current and historical)
     * Immunization Records
     * Laboratory Results (blood tests, imaging, pathology)
     * Vital Signs (blood pressure, heart rate, temperature, weight)
     * Clinical Notes (physician observations, treatment plans)
     * Surgical History
     * Family Medical History
     * Genetic Information (if available)
   
   - Treatment Data:
     * Procedures Performed
     * Prescriptions Written
     * Referrals to Specialists
     * Hospital Admissions and Discharges
     * Emergency Department Visits
   
   Legal Basis: Treatment, Payment, Healthcare Operations (HIPAA)
   Retention Period: Minimum 7 years, longer for minors (state-dependent)

2. CLAIMS DATA WAREHOUSE (CLAIMS-DB)
   Location: AWS HIPAA-compliant environment (us-east-1)
   Purpose: Store insurance claims and billing data
   PHI Processed:
   - Patient ID (unique identifier)
   - Member ID (insurance identifier)
   - Date of Service
   - Diagnosis Codes (ICD-10)
   - Procedure Codes (CPT, HCPCS)
   - Provider Information (NPI, specialty)
   - Claim Amounts (billed, allowed, paid)
   - Pharmacy Claims (medications, dosages, costs)
   - Prior Authorization Records
   
   Legal Basis: Payment, Healthcare Operations (HIPAA)
   Retention Period: 10 years (CMS requirement)

3. PATIENT PORTAL DATABASE (PATIENT-PORTAL)
   Provider: MyChart (Epic patient portal)
   Location: On-premise data center
   Purpose: Enable patient access to health records and communication
   PHI Collected:
   - Patient Login Activity (timestamps, IP addresses)
   - Portal Messages (patient-provider communications)
   - Appointment Requests and Scheduling
   - Prescription Refill Requests
   - Medical Record Downloads (audit trail)
   - Patient-Reported Outcomes (surveys, symptom tracking)
   - Proxy Access Records (family member access)
   
   Legal Basis: Treatment, Patient Access Rights (HIPAA)
   Retention Period: Aligned with EHR retention

4. REMOTE PATIENT MONITORING SYSTEM (RPM-PLATFORM)
   Provider: Philips HealthSuite
   Location: Cloud (HIPAA-compliant, US data centers)
   Purpose: Collect data from home monitoring devices
   PHI Collected:
   - Patient ID
   - Device Data:
     * Blood Glucose Readings (for diabetic patients)
     * Blood Pressure Readings (for hypertensive patients)
     * Weight Measurements (for heart failure patients)
     * Oxygen Saturation (SpO2) (for respiratory patients)
     * Heart Rate and ECG Data (for cardiac patients)
     * Activity Levels (step counts, exercise duration)
     * Sleep Patterns (duration, quality)
   - Device Compliance (usage frequency, adherence)
   - Alert Thresholds and Triggered Alerts
   
   Legal Basis: Treatment (HIPAA)
   Consent: Explicit patient consent for RPM program
   Retention Period: Duration of monitoring + 7 years

5. WEARABLE DEVICE DATA INTEGRATION (WEARABLE-API)
   Provider: Apple HealthKit, Google Fit, Fitbit API
   Location: Cloud (vendor-specific)
   Purpose: Integrate patient-owned wearable device data
   PHI Collected:
   - Patient ID (linked to EHR)
   - Health Metrics:
     * Daily Step Count
     * Heart Rate (resting, active, variability)
     * Sleep Data (duration, stages)
     * Exercise Sessions (type, duration, intensity)
     * Calories Burned
     * Menstrual Cycle Data (if tracked)
     * Blood Oxygen Levels
   - Device Type and Model
   
   Legal Basis: Patient Authorization (HIPAA), Consent
   Opt-in: Patients explicitly authorize data sharing
   Retention Period: Duration of authorization + 7 years

6. PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS ENGINE (HEALTH-ML)
   Location: Azure Machine Learning (HIPAA-compliant)
   Purpose: Generate predictive models for clinical decision support
   PHI Processed:
   - De-identified Patient Cohorts (for model training)
   - Identified Patient Data (for individual predictions):
     * Patient ID
     * All clinical data from EHR
     * Claims history
     * Social determinants of health
     * Wearable device data
   
   Predictions Generated:
   - Hospital Readmission Risk (30-day, 90-day)
   - Disease Progression Likelihood
   - Medication Adherence Probability
   - Emergency Department Visit Risk
   - Chronic Disease Development Risk
   - Treatment Response Predictions
   
   Legal Basis: Healthcare Operations (HIPAA)
   Automated Decision: Clinical decision support only (not sole basis for treatment)
   Human Review: All predictions reviewed by clinicians

7. SOCIAL DETERMINANTS OF HEALTH DATABASE (SDOH-DB)
   Location: On-premise data center
   Purpose: Store non-clinical factors affecting health outcomes
   PHI Collected:
   - Patient ID
   - Social Factors:
     * Housing Status (stable, unstable, homeless)
     * Food Security (access to nutritious food)
     * Transportation Access (ability to attend appointments)
     * Social Support Network (family, community)
     * Education Level
     * Employment Status and Occupation
     * Income Level (estimated or self-reported)
     * Insurance Status and Type
   - Environmental Factors:
     * Neighborhood Safety
     * Access to Healthcare Facilities
     * Environmental Exposures
   
   Legal Basis: Treatment, Healthcare Operations (HIPAA)
   Collection Method: Patient surveys, community health workers
   Sensitivity: High - potential for discrimination if misused

8. POPULATION HEALTH MANAGEMENT PLATFORM (PHM-SYSTEM)
   Provider: Arcadia Analytics
   Location: Cloud (HIPAA-compliant)
   Purpose: Aggregate and analyze population-level health data
   PHI Processed:
   - All data from EHR-MAIN, CLAIMS-DB, RPM-PLATFORM
   - Aggregated by:
     * Geographic Region
     * Diagnosis/Condition
     * Risk Stratification Level
     * Provider/Clinic
   
   Analytics:
   - Care Gap Identification (overdue screenings, vaccinations)
   - High-Risk Patient Identification
   - Quality Measure Reporting (HEDIS, CMS Stars)
   - Cost and Utilization Trends
   - Outcome Tracking
   
   Legal Basis: Healthcare Operations (HIPAA)
   Reporting: Aggregated data for quality improvement

9. CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT SYSTEM (CDS-ENGINE)
   Integrated with: EHR-MAIN
   Purpose: Provide real-time clinical recommendations
   PHI Used:
   - Current Patient Context (diagnosis, medications, allergies)
   - Predictive Risk Scores from HEALTH-ML
   - Evidence-Based Guidelines
   - Drug Interaction Databases
   
   Alerts Generated:
   - Drug-Drug Interactions
   - Drug-Allergy Alerts
   - Duplicate Therapy Warnings
   - Preventive Care Reminders
   - High-Risk Patient Flags
   
   Legal Basis: Treatment (HIPAA)
   Override: Clinicians can override with documentation

10. RESEARCH DATA REPOSITORY (RESEARCH-DB)
    Location: Secure research environment (on-premise)
    Purpose: Support clinical research and quality improvement
    PHI Status: De-identified or Limited Data Set
    Data Included:
    - De-identified clinical data
    - Dates of service (limited data set)
    - Geographic information (ZIP code only)
    - Ages (including over 89)
    
    Legal Basis: Research (IRB-approved), Healthcare Operations
    Access: Restricted to approved researchers
    Safeguards: Data Use Agreements, IRB oversight

PROCESS FLOW
------------

Step 1: Data Ingestion
- EHR-MAIN continuously updated by clinicians
- CLAIMS-DB receives claims data from payers (daily batch)
- RPM-PLATFORM receives device data (real-time)
- WEARABLE-API pulls data from patient wearables (hourly)
- SDOH-DB updated via patient surveys and community health workers
- Purpose: Maintain comprehensive patient data

Step 2: Data Integration
- PHM-SYSTEM aggregates data from all sources
- Patient ID used as master identifier
- Data normalized and standardized
- Duplicate records resolved
- Purpose: Create unified patient view

Step 3: Data Quality Checks
- Automated validation rules applied
- Missing data flagged
- Outliers identified for review
- Inconsistencies resolved
- Purpose: Ensure data accuracy for analytics

Step 4: Risk Stratification
- HEALTH-ML calculates risk scores for all patients
- Patients categorized: Low, Medium, High, Very High risk
- Risk factors identified for each patient
- Purpose: Prioritize care management resources

Step 5: Care Gap Identification
- PHM-SYSTEM identifies overdue preventive services
- Compares patient data to quality measures
- Generates care gap reports by patient and provider
- Purpose: Improve quality of care, close gaps

Step 6: Predictive Modeling
- HEALTH-ML generates predictions for high-risk patients:
  * Hospital readmission probability
  * Disease progression timeline
  * Medication adherence likelihood
- Models updated monthly with new data
- Purpose: Enable proactive interventions

Step 7: Care Team Notification
- High-risk patients flagged in EHR-MAIN
- Care managers receive patient lists
- Alerts sent for critical predictions (e.g., high readmission risk)
- Purpose: Trigger care management interventions

Step 8: Intervention Planning
- Care managers review patient data
- Develop personalized care plans
- Coordinate with providers
- Interventions may include:
  * Medication reconciliation
  * Home health visits
  * Transportation assistance
  * Social services referrals
  * Patient education
- Purpose: Prevent adverse outcomes

Step 9: Outcome Tracking
- Patient outcomes monitored in PHM-SYSTEM
- Intervention effectiveness measured
- Readmissions, ED visits, costs tracked
- Purpose: Evaluate program success, refine models

Step 10: Quality Reporting
- Aggregated data reported to:
  * CMS (Medicare quality measures)
  * Health plans (HEDIS measures)
  * State health departments
  * Internal quality committees
- Purpose: Demonstrate quality, identify improvement opportunities

Step 11: Research and Innovation
- De-identified data exported to RESEARCH-DB
- Researchers analyze for new insights
- Findings published (no PHI disclosed)
- Models improved based on research
- Purpose: Advance medical knowledge

DATA SHARING AND TRANSFERS
---------------------------

Internal Sharing:
- Clinicians: Access to patient data for treatment
- Care Managers: Access to assigned patient data
- Billing: Access to demographic and insurance data
- Quality Team: Access to aggregated quality metrics
- Research Team: Access to de-identified data only

External Sharing (HIPAA-Compliant):
- Health Plans: Claims data, quality measures (Business Associate Agreement)
- Specialists: Referral information, clinical summaries (Treatment)
- Laboratories: Orders and results (Treatment)
- Pharmacies: Prescriptions (Treatment)
- Public Health Authorities: Reportable diseases, immunizations (Legal Requirement)
- CMS: Quality reporting (Legal Requirement)

Patient-Directed Sharing:
- Patient Authorization: Share records with other providers, family members
- Patient Portal: Patients download their own records
- Right to Request Restrictions: Patients can limit certain disclosures

No Sharing Without Authorization:
- Marketing purposes (prohibited)
- Sale of PHI (prohibited)
- Employers (without authorization)
- Life insurers (without authorization)

International Transfers:
- None - all data stored in US
- For EU patients: GDPR compliance, explicit consent

PATIENT RIGHTS (HIPAA)
----------------------

Right of Access:
- Patients can obtain copy of their medical records
- Response time: 30 days (extendable to 60 days)
- Format: Electronic or paper, as requested
- Fee: Reasonable cost-based fee allowed

Right to Amend:
- Patients can request corrections to their records
- Provider may deny if information is accurate
- Patient can submit statement of disagreement

Right to Accounting of Disclosures:
- Patients can request list of disclosures (past 6 years)
- Excludes: Treatment, payment, operations, patient-authorized

Right to Request Restrictions:
- Patients can request limits on uses and disclosures
- Provider not required to agree (except for self-pay restriction)

Right to Confidential Communications:
- Patients can request alternative contact methods
- Must accommodate reasonable requests

Right to Notification of Breach:
- Patients notified if PHI breach occurs
- Notification within 60 days of discovery

SECURITY MEASURES (HIPAA Security Rule)
----------------------------------------

Administrative Safeguards:
- Security Officer designated
- Workforce training on HIPAA
- Access authorization procedures
- Incident response procedures
- Business Associate Agreements with all vendors
- Regular risk assessments

Physical Safeguards:
- Facility access controls (badge system)
- Workstation security (locked screens)
- Device and media controls (encryption, disposal)
- Secure data center (24/7 monitoring)

Technical Safeguards:
- Access controls (unique user IDs, automatic logoff)
- Audit controls (all access logged)
- Integrity controls (data not altered)
- Transmission security (encryption in transit - TLS 1.3)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)

COMPLIANCE NOTES
----------------

HIPAA Privacy Rule:
- Minimum necessary standard applied
- Notice of Privacy Practices provided to all patients
- Patient authorizations obtained when required
- Disclosures tracked and logged

HIPAA Security Rule:
- Risk assessment completed annually
- Security policies and procedures documented
- Technical safeguards implemented
- Workforce trained on security

HITECH Act:
- Breach notification procedures in place
- Business Associate Agreements with all vendors
- Accounting of disclosures for EHR access
- Patient access to EHR data

GDPR (for EU patients):
- Legal basis: Consent, Vital interests, Legal obligation
- Data minimization applied
- Right to erasure (subject to legal retention)
- Data Protection Impact Assessment completed

- California CMIA: Additional protections for medical information

- Massachusetts: Encryption requirements

42 CFR Part 2 (Substance Use Disorder):
- Separate consent for substance use treatment records
- Stricter disclosure requirements
- Special protections for SUD data

RISKS AND MITIGATION
--------------------

Risk: Unauthorized access to PHI
Mitigation: Access controls, audit logging, workforce training, sanctions

Risk: Data breach exposing patient information
Mitigation: Encryption, security assessments, incident response plan, insurance

Risk: Algorithmic bias in predictive models
Mitigation: Fairness audits, diverse training data, human oversight, transparency

Risk: Discrimination based on SDOH data
Mitigation: Access restrictions, training, policies prohibiting discrimination

Risk: Patient privacy concerns limiting data sharing
Mitigation: Transparency, patient education, opt-in for non-essential uses

Risk: Non-compliance with HIPAA
Mitigation: Compliance program, regular audits, training, legal review

Risk: Inaccurate predictions leading to inappropriate care
Mitigation: Clinical validation, human review, continuous model monitoring
